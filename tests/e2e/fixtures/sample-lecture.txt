Unit 1: Introduction to Biochemistry

Biochemistry is the study of chemical processes within and relating to living organisms.
It bridges biology and chemistry by examining how biological molecules give rise to the
processes that occur within living cells.

Key topics covered in this unit:
- The four main classes of biomolecules: carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, and nucleic acids.
- Water as the universal solvent and its role in biological systems.
- pH, buffers, and the importance of maintaining physiological conditions.
- The structure and function of amino acids and peptide bonds.

By the end of this unit, students should be able to identify the major classes of
biomolecules and explain why water is essential to life.
